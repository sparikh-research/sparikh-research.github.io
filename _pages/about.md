I am a mathematically inclined Machine Learning researcher currently pursuing my **MSc in Computer Science** at **Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich (LMU)**. 

My research interests lie at the intersection of:
* **Representation Learning** (Joint-Embedding Predictive Architectures, Self-Supervised Learning)
* **Mechanistic Interpretability** (Internal representation probing, latent alignments, activation steering)
* **Mathematical Foundations of ML** (Information geometry, learning theory, generalization dynamics)
* **Formal Verification** (Interactive theorem proving with Lean)
* **AI for Science & Structural Biology** (Protein language models, algorithmic bioinformatics)

## Current Flagship Project

### **Hierarchical Predictive World Models for Surgical Video** (MSc Thesis)
I am developing hierarchical predictive world models to reason over complex, multi-granular dynamics in surgical videos. By utilizing predictive representation learning and state abstraction, this project aims to build robust world models that avoid the pixel-space reconstruction failure modes common to traditional generative models.

## Active Research

### **Research Assistant (Hiwi) — Machine Learning for Binary Analysis**
*Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich (Apr 2026 – Sep 2026)*

I run two core research tracks in cross-modal representation learning:
1. **Representation Learning & Distillation:** Developing a Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA) for cross-modal mapping, and distilling a 1.3B parameter LLM into a 6× smaller encoder while preserving state-of-the-art performance on assembly code representations.
2. **Mechanistic Interpretability:** Probing cross-modal attention mechanisms and latent alignments to extract human-understandable semantics and verify structural preservation of binary control flow graphs.
